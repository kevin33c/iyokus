    In which cases I can't return an item?

    An item is not elegible for refund if any of below criteria apply:

    • You already rated the seller.
    • The item is on transit to you.
    • For an used item, the delivery of the order took place more than 24 hours ago.
    • For a new item, the delivery of the order took place more than 14 days ago.
    • The item is not in the same condition as when it was delivered.

    Can I cancel an order before delivery?

    No, on Iyokus, once the product is paid and dispatched, is no longer possible to cancel the order. However, in special cases you can send us an email to client@iyokus.com.

    Buyer guidelines

    We work hard to make Iyokus an easy to use and safe platform. We proctect our buyers from frauds and guarantee refunds. But we can’t do it alone.

    When buying, please keep in mind the following:

    • Do not buy any products from the Prohibited Items list (we'll periodically take down prohibited items on our site but we, nonetheless, advice basic common sense to our buyers)
    • Read the product description carefully, and pay special attention to the product's delivery time and conditions
    • Upon receiving the item, examine it immediately and do not rate the seller until you are satisfied with the item
    • For Meetup Delivery, do not give the Payment Code to the seller without examining the item first and follow the Safety Guidelines
    • For new items, make sure to claim your refund within 14 days
    • For used items, make sure to claim your refund within 24 hours
    • Do not use or damage the item and keep, where applicable, the original packaging if you plan to ask for a refund

    How long does it take to get a refund?

    It usually takes 3-5 working days to reach your account once the seller has confirmed that the item was sent back. Note that this time may vary depending on your bank/card provider, however, we'll process the refund within 1 working day.

If that card has been canceled or replaced, you will need to first contact your card issuer for further assistance in tracking the refund. Typically, your card issuer will accept the refund on your behalf and either process it into your current account or wait for you to contact them to collect the funds.

    In the unlikely case that this is not possible, please contact us as soon as possible and we'll look into it for you or advice you on how to proceed.

    How does the delivery process work for a seller?

    Iyokus offers two delivery methods, "Meetup Delivery" and "Postal Delivery"

    Remember, if you are a private seller, you are only able to deliver via "Meetup Delivery".


    "Meetup Delivery" : Contact the buyer to agree on a delivery time and location. You can contact the buyer from your order page in My Orders section.

    1. Organize the meeting point for the delivery with the buyer
    2. Meet the buyer and deliver the product
    3. Receive the 6 digits Payment Code from the buyer.
    4. In your account, go to the order page and enter the Payment Code received.
    5. Upon fulfilment confirmation and if we don't receive a claim/refund request witin 24 hours, we will transfer the money to your account in 24 hours. Note, depending on your banking provider it may takes 1-3 working days to appear on your account.

    "Postal Delivery" : send the product to the provided address. You can manage your orders in My Orders section.

    1. Send the order to the provided address within 2 working day. Remember to include an invoice and a the return address in case the buyer wish to request a refund.
    2. Once dispatched, go to the order page in My Orders section to change the status to "Dispatched". Please indicate the delivery company and the tracking number.
    3. We'll send a dispatch confirmation to the buyer.
    4. The payment will be transferred to your account in 14 days if the buyer does not request a refund. Note, depending on your banking provider it may takes 1-3 working days to appear on your account.

    What is the main difference between a private/individual and a business/professional seller?

    Generally speaking, a private account is for someone who sells occasionally. And a business account is for online shops, businesses or someone who sells frequently.

    The main operational difference between a business seller and a private seller is that the private seller can only deliver products using "Meetup Delivery". Therefore, a private seller is limited to sell only to users living within the same city/region.

    A business seller does not have this limitation and is able to choose between "Postal Delivery" and "Meetup Delivery". Therefore, a business seller is able to sell anywhere within Iyokus operational range (i.e. currently is within the United Kingdom but we are looking to expand further).

    Product listing tips & guidelines

    Uploading a product listing is relatively easy. We recommend to follow these steps before uploading a product:

    1. Familiar yourself with the uploading platform and the information required (e.g. understand what are lock-out period, reserve price, delivery methods, etc.)
    2. Have at least 1 up to date picture of your product ready
    3. Fill out the uploading form even with just dummy information for the moment to complete the upload, don't worry it'll not be visible yet and you can always edit it
    4. Complete all the information required truthfully and correctly. Remember that the buyers reserve the right to refunds if the item isn't as advertised
    5. Always preview the listing before publishing it
    6. Publish the item and update periodically (e.g. quantity available, prices, etc.) as required

    Remember to publish the item once you have added and reviewed it. Otherwise, it will not be visible to the buyers.

    Lock-out period guide

    The lockout period is the period, measured in number of days, where a buyer will not be able to bid again for a particular product. The lock-out period is applied, and specific to the affected product only, when the initial bid sent by a buyer is rejected or if the buyer reject the counter-offer.

    The lock-out period is required to ensure that a buyer does not bid incrementally low prices until the bid is accepted. Sellers are able to determine the duration of the lock-out period each time they upload a product and can be changed thereafter by editing the listing.

    The lock-out period has the effect that positively encourage serious bids from buyers. A longer lock-out period should attract more serious bids but lower the total number of bids received, and vice versa. A seller should tailor a lock-period to his/her needs and wants, we, however, generally recommend a minimum lock-period of 5 days.
