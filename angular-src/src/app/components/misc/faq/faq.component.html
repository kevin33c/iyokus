What is Iyokus?

Iyokus is an e-commerce platform that connects buyer and seller at the best price of mutual satisfaction. It gives the buyers the pricing initiative and provides the mean to express their willingness to pay to the seller in the form of a bid price for an instantaneous feedback.

What makes Iyokus different from other e-commerce platforms?

We proudly distinguish ourselves from most of other e-commerce sites, where users must decide whether to pay a list price in order to purchase the product they want, by giving them the additional opportunity of bidding their own price. We are also different from e-commerce sites operating an auction model in that we do not pitch buyers' bids against each other.

In other words, we enable a buyer to bid a price of his or her liking for a product that he or she wants to purchase in addition to the usual possibility of purchasing it at list price. Unlike a rigid or fixed pricing system, our pricing model benefits both sellers, as they gain access to more willing buyers, and buyers, as they gain more pricing power and initiative as well as the chance of getting a better price.

Iyokus Bidding System

How does the Iyokus Bidding System work?

Iyokus operates what we call a "Two-Stage Bidding System".

In stage one you bid your price and one of the following can happen:
(1) Your bid is accepted and you can proceed to payment
(2) Your bid is rejected
(3) You receive a counter-offer

If you receive a counter-offer (3), then you enter the stage two of the system and one of the following can happen:
(4) You accept the counter-offer and you can proceed to payment
(5) You reject the counter-offer and bid failed

If your bid is accepted (1) or if you accept the counter-offer (4), you will have 24 hours (meanwhile offer remains subject to availability) to complete the payment otherwise the offer will become invalid.

If your bid is rejected (2) or if you reject the counter-offer (5), you will enter a "lock-out period" during which you will not able to bid again for the product. But remember you can always pay the list price to purchase the product.

What price should I bid?

We highly recommend you bid the price that you truly value the product you want to purchase. You should always consider the following principles when you bidding a price:

(1) Bid a price below list price, obviously
(2) If you bid above your true price you may regret paying too much
(3) If you bid below your true price you may not able to purchase the product and enter a lock-out period, during the which the product could run out of stock
(4) Only if you bid truthfully you will be buying without regret. Since if your bid is accepted you will be paying exactly the amount you are willing to buy and if your bid is rejected at least you will not have to over pay.

When I place a bid, am I committing to buy the product?

No, whether your bid is accepted or if you accept the counter offer, in neither case you are committed to pay for the product. However, you will have only 24 hours to complete the payment otherwise the offer will expire and you will enter automatically into a lock out period, during which you will not able to bid again for the same product.

How long do I have to wait before I know whether the bid is accepted or not?

Almost instantaneously, within seconds you will receive an answer from our pricing algorithm and know whether your bid is accepted, rejected or countered.

Is the same bid always accepted or rejected?

No, same bid for the same product may receive different outcomes. Our pricing system takes into consideration a variety of factors (i.e. inventory, demand, etc.) therefore outcome may be different for different users even when bidding the same price for the same product. Offers and counter offers may vary across time and users.

What is a counter-offer?

A counter offer is a system generated alternative price offer to your initial bid. A counter offer is generated if the system decides that your bid too low but consider your willingness to pay not too far away from a price of mutual satisfaction.

Will I always receive a counter-offer?

No, system does not always generate a counter offer. Your bid could be rejected without a counter offer if it is too far away from a price that the seller is willing to accept.

Will everyone receive the same counter-offer if they bid the same price?

No, many factors are taken into account when generating counter-offers, therefore, these may be different across time and users even for the same bid and same product.

What is a lock-out period?

A period after which your bid failed and during which you will not be allowed to bid again for the same product. Can vary across products.

What is the duration of the lock-out period?

A lock out period duration is decided by the seller of the specific product. It could be from days to indefinite. You will be warned and notified of the duration of the lock-out period before you submit your bid.

Can I still buy the product if I am in a lock-out period?

Yes, remember you can always pay list price to purchase the product even during a lock out period.

When can I bid again for a product if my bid failed?

After the lock-out period expires.
